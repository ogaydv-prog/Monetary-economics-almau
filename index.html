Course Syllabus

SEF 1313: Monetary Economics (5 ECTS)

Monetary Economics is a branch of economics that studies various theories of money: it provides a basis for analyzing the money supply and considers its functions, as well as international aspects related to the circulation and exchange of foreign currency for international trade.

Course Learning Outcomes (LOs):

  • LO1: Understand the role of money in the economy and how central banks use monetary policy instruments (interest rates, open market operations) to stabilize the economy.
  • LO2: Explain the causes of inflation and deflation and propose potential control measures and their economic consequences.
  • LO3: Analyze how changes in monetary policy affect macroeconomic indicators such as GDP, employment, and exchange rates.
  • LO4: Evaluate the interaction and potential conflicts between fiscal and monetary policies in achieving economic goals.
  • LO5: Understand the impact of monetary policy on global processes, including exchange rate fluctuations and international capital movements.

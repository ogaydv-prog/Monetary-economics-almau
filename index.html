Course Syllabus (Official AlmaU Document)

5 ECTS

1. Course Description

Monetary Economics studies various theories of money, providing a robust basis for analyzing the money supply, monetary transmission mechanisms, and international tie-ins related to monetary policy and global capital flows.

2. Learning Outcomes (LO)

  • LO1: Understand the role of money and how central banks utilize monetary policy instruments to stabilize output and prices.
  • LO2: Analyze the structural causes of inflation/deflation and propose strategic policy counters.
  • LO3: Map the transmission of monetary policy changes into macroeconomic aggregates (GDP, exchange rates).
  • LO4: Evaluate fiscal-monetary coordination and policy conflicts.
  • LO5: Understand the impact of international capital movements and exchange rate regimes (The Impossible Trilemma).

3. Weekly Plan

Week 1: Intro to Monetary Economics, Functions of Money & Demand for Money.
Week 2: Money Supply, Liquidity, and Inflation as a Financial Asset.
Week 3-4: Central Banking & Monetary Policy: Goals, Instruments & Transmission.
Week 5: Commercial Banks, Banking System, and Deposit Creation.
Week 6: Macroeconomic Effects & Money Multipliers. Introduction to QPM modeling.
Week 7: Introduction to the QPM: General Characteristics, Calibration and Midterm.

Milton Friedman's restated demand formulation of cash assets as a specific asset held on consumer utility matrices.

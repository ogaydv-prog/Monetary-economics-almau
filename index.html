Course Syllabus

SEF 1313: Monetary Economics (5 ECTS)

Monetary Economics is a branch of economics that studies various theories of money: it provides a basis for analyzing the money supply and considers its functions, as well as international aspects related to the circulation and exchange of foreign currency for international trade.

Course Learning Outcomes (LOs):

  • LO1: Understand the role of money in the economy and how central banks use monetary policy instruments to stabilize the economy.
  • LO2: Explain the causes of inflation and deflation and propose potential control measures.
  • LO3: Analyze how changes in monetary policy affect macroeconomic indicators such as GDP and employment.
  • LO4: Evaluate the interaction between fiscal and monetary policies.
  • LO5: Understand the impact of monetary policy on global processes (Impossible Trilemma).

Required Academic Readings

Paper 1

M. Friedman: "The Role of Monetary Policy" (1968)

Establishing the natural rate of unemployment and limits of policy.

Paper 2

J. Taylor: "Discretion vs Policy Rules" (1993)

The seminal publication introducing the Taylor Rule reaction function.

Paper 3

R. Mundell: "Capital Mobility and Size" (1963)

Foundational work on the Impossible Trilemma.

Paper 4

IMF: "FPAS and QPM Framework"

Forecasting and Policy Analysis Systems used by Central Banks.

Paper 5

B. Bernanke: "The Macroeconomics of the Great Depression"

Analyzing the failure of the banking system and money supply collapse.

Paper 6

BIS: "Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs)"

The future of fiat money and wholesale settlement systems.

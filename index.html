Course Description

This course systematically explores the channels of monetary transmission, the operational balance sheet dynamics of modern central banking, and macroeconomic trade-offs captured in the Impossible Trilemma. It is structured around the Mishkin framework and the IMF's semi-structural QPM forecasting approach. Students will learn how central banks utilize open market operations and interest rates to manage inflation and stabilize output.

Learning Outcomes (LOs)

LO 1: Liquidity & Policy

Evaluate how monetary policy instruments impact commercial bank liquidity and target money supply.

LO 2: Interest Rates

Apply the Fisher Equation and Bond Pricing theory to structural interest rate fluctuations.

LO 3: Balance Sheets

Analyze open-market structural adjustments utilizing Central Bank T-Accounts.

LO 4: Taylor Rule

Synthesize policy decisions using the Taylor Rule response function and QPM modeling.

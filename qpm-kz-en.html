Model Methodology & Equations (incl. Expectations & Kalman Filter)

1. Phillips Curve (Inflation):
π(t) = λ·E[π] + (1-λ)·π(t-1) + α·y(t-1) + β·Δz(t) + ε_π
Inflation depends on agents' inflation expectations E[π] (forward-looking), past inflation (inertia), the output gap (overheating), and exchange rate pass-through. Adding the expectation weight λ brings the simulator closer to actual QPM models.

2. IS Curve (Aggregate Demand):
y(t) = γ·y(t-1) - δ·(r(t-1) - r*) + ρ·Oil + ε_y
The output gap y(t) depends on the monetary policy stance (deviation of the real rate r from the neutral rate r*). Oil prices act as an exogenous stimulus for a commodity-exporting economy.

3. Taylor Rule & Kalman Filter:
i(t) = r* + π(t) + c1·(π(t) - π*) + c2·ŷ(t)
The central bank sets the policy rate i(t). However, it does not observe the actual output gap instantaneously! Instead, it uses the Kalman filter estimate ŷ(t). The filter iteratively updates the state estimate based on noisy signals, creating a lag in the policymaker's reaction.

4. Uncovered Interest Parity (UIP):
z(t) = z(t-1) - (r(t) - r_foreign) + ε_z(Oil)
The real exchange rate appreciates with a high interest rate and rising oil prices (Dutch disease). Currency depreciation (increase in z) leads to imported inflation in Equation 1.
